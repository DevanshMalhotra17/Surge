Flood Response Guide

Survive During The Rising Waters.

When floodwater arrives, every second counts. Know the rules that save lives — what to do, what to avoid, and how to stay alive if you're trapped.

TURN AROUND, DON'T DROWN — Never walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters. It is the #1 cause of flood deaths.
Critical Knowledge

How Dangerous Is The Water?

Floodwater is deceptively powerful. Here's what each depth can do.

6"
Inches of Water
Enough to knock an adult off their feet. Moving water at this depth carries significant force. Never attempt to walk through it.
12"
Inches of Water
Can float most vehicles. Your car becomes a death trap — tires lose traction and the current takes control. Abandon the vehicle.
24"
Inches of Water
Can carry away SUVs, trucks, and large vehicles. At this depth, rescue becomes extremely difficult. Evacuate before it reaches this point.
Step 01 — Immediate Actions

Do's & Don'ts

Knowing what NOT to do is just as important as knowing what to do.

✓ Do This
Move immediately to higher ground. Don't wait for official orders if you see rising water.
Listen to NOAA Weather Radio or local emergency broadcasts for evacuation instructions.
If ordered to evacuate — leave immediately. Take your go-bag and follow your planned route.
Turn off utilities (gas, electricity) at the main switches if you have time and it's safe to do so.
If trapped in a building, move to the highest floor. Signal for help from a window or rooftop.
✕ Never Do This
Never walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters. You can't judge the depth or what's beneath.
Don't climb into a closed attic — you could become trapped by rising water with no escape.
Don't touch downed power lines or electrical equipment in or near floodwater.
Don't return to your home until authorities confirm it's safe. There may be structural damage.
Don't use generators or grills indoors — carbon monoxide kills silently.
